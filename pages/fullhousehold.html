The total land area of the UK is 24.361 million hectares. Divided by the total number of households in the UK this works out to being 9370 m2 per household. The ZeroCarbonBritain scenario allocates 3186 m2 (34%) for food production, 3092 m2 (33%) for carbon capture, 937 m2 (10%) for conservation, 562 m2 (6%) urban, leaving 1593 m2 (17%) for fuel and energy.

A more advanced model of industrial demand worth developing in future would include an analysis of the main types of goods produced and larger energy consuming processes such as aluminium and steel and concrete production, this could be tied into an analysis of the embodied energy that is required to produce many of the technologies included in this model which cover building construction, household appliances and cars.
